Oxygen Loss
The muscles in your neck tense, and churn, your diaphragm writhes. Your face feels hot, pink, and saliva is pooling by your esophagus. Satisfaction seems an alien planet. Struggling resignation feels like home. You mouth chews dumbly. A tingle rises along the inside of each arm, a gray vignette pulls at the edge of your vision, both growing with each thump of your haggard heart. A moment of dizziness bowls you over - perhaps you crash to the ground, but of that, you are unaware. All that remains is the fuzzy grayness, and even that is descending away from you. Your vision is a tight, milky cone. And then it is gone.
