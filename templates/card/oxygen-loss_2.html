Oxygen Loss
Sip each tiny breath, the burps of cool, teal air finding their way to desperate, blackening lungs. Your lips are witted with the anticipation, Every breath is a poltergeist: it brigs sensation, but is unable to be grasped. The Norns are undoing the world, pulling at the threads. Sound is replaced with distant siren-song. Reality softens, and frays. You fumble, tumble, your legs weakly informing you that you must be standing on sand. But no - there is the ground, greeting you hard and sudden. The bruising fall feels somehow muffled, the asphalt like thick, warm velvet beneath you. There is a last moment of lucidity, of panic, of eyes-wide, leg-kicking, rabid-rabid fight. It is quickly subsumed by the yawning void.
